A detailed comparison of in-game statistics for the different ships in Elite

Here's a comparison of all the different ships in Elite, using the values from within the source code. Click on the table headers to sort by that specification. The table shows the values from the BBC Master version of Elite, and footnotes point out any variations in the other versions.

This table only includes ships that you will meet in-flight and in the official game. It doesn't include the subtly different ship blueprints from the docked code in the BBC Micro disc version, as these are only used when displaying the ship hangar). It also omits the Elite logo from the 6502 Second Processor version, as this only appears in the demo. And it doesn't include any data from Elite-A, as the ship specifications are really quite different in that version.

A lot of this data comes from the ship blueprints, with other data coming from the scacol, shpcol, KWH% and E% tables, and spawning code in part 1, part 3 and part 4 of the main game loop.

Note that the table is pretty wide - to see all the different versions, you may need to scroll the window to the right.

Footnotes
---------

  1. ^ Ship IDs are different in the BBC Micro cassette and Acorn Electron versions (see the deep dive on ship blueprints for details).
  2. ^ The maximum edge count does not include the extra edge that is used to store the ship colour in the 6502 Second Processor and BBC Master versions.
  3. ^ Fractional kill points are supported in the Commodore 64, Apple II, BBC Master and NES versions. All other versions award one point for each kill, regardless of the target. See the deep dive on combat rank for details.
  4. ^ Only the 6502 Second Processor and BBC Master versions have differently coloured ships; all other versions have monochrome space views.
  5. ^ This is how I classify the different versions of Elite for the purposes of this table:
    • Standard ships appear in the BBC Micro cassette version, plus all enhanced and advanced versions.
    • Enhanced ships appear in the BBC Micro disc version, plus all advanced versions.
    • Advanced ships appear in the 6502 Second Processor, Commodore 64, Apple II, BBC Master and NES versions.
  6. ^ Adders in the BBC Micro disc version have a visibility distance of 23.
  7. ^ Anacondas in the BBC Micro disc version have a visibility distance of 50.
  8. ^ The Cougar is ship 33 in the 6502 Second Processor version, as the Elite logo is ship 32.
  9. ^ The Cougar is not included in the Apple II version.
  10. ^ The Dodo station is ship 34 in the 6502 Second Processor version, as the Elite logo is ship 32 and the Cougar is ship 33.
  11. ^ Dodo stations in the NES version have an explosion count of 0, though this makes no difference as stations can't explode.
  12. ^ Escape pods in the BBC Micro cassette and Acorn Electron versions store their faces with a scale factor of 8.
  13. ^ Kraits in the BBC Micro disc version have a visibility distance of 25.
  14. ^ All Pythons in the BBC Micro cassette and Acorn Electron versions have a bounty of 200 Cr, whereas other versions have two types of Python: traders (with no bounty) and pirates (with a bounty of 200 Cr).
  15. ^ Pythons in the BBC Micro cassette and Acorn Electron versions have a larger targetable area (120 x 120) than in all other versions (80 x 80).
  16. ^ Pythons in the BBC Micro cassette and Acorn Electron versions spawn up to 3 canisters on their demise, but this rises to 5 canisters in all other versions.
  17. ^ Pythons in the BBC Micro cassette and Acorn Electron versions have a slightly more glorious demise, with 10 expoding nodes compared to 9 nodes in all other versions.
  18. ^ Vipers have a different maximum energy in the BBC Micro disc version (100), and in the BBC Micro cassette and Acorn Electron versions (120). This means they are easiest to kill in the disc version, harder to kill in the cassette and Electron versions, and even harder to kill in the advanced versions.
